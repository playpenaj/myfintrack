70-20-10 Budget Rule

The 70-20-10 rule is a simple budgeting framework that helps you allocate your income effectively for financial stability and growth.

Spending (70%)

This portion of your income should cover all your essential and discretionary expenses:

  • Housing: Rent, mortgage, property taxes
  • Utilities: Electricity, water, internet, phone
  • Food: Groceries and dining out
  • Transportation: Public transport, fuel, car maintenance
  • Healthcare: Medical expenses, insurance
  • Personal Care: Toiletries, grooming
  • Entertainment: Leisure activities, hobbies
  • Shopping: Clothing, household items

Savings (20%)

This portion is dedicated to building financial security:

  • Emergency Fund: 3-6 months of living expenses
  • Debt Payments: Credit cards, loans (beyond minimum payments)
  • Sinking Funds: Planned future expenses (vacations, gifts, repairs)
  • Short-term Goals: Down payments, large purchases

Investments (10%)

This portion focuses on long-term wealth building:

  • Retirement Accounts: CPF, SRS, pension plans
  • Stocks & ETFs: Equity investments for growth
  • Real Estate: Property investments
  • Education: Skills development, courses
  • Business Ventures: Side businesses or investments
  • Emergency Fund: Long-term security buffer (recommended: 3-6 months of essential expenses)

Emergency Fund Recommendation

We recommend building an emergency fund of S$26,100 to S$52,200 (3-6 months of expenses).

You can start by allocating S$500-1,000 monthly to your emergency fund until you reach your target.

How to Implement

1. Calculate your monthly after-tax income

2. Allocate 70% to spending categories

3. Dedicate 20% to savings goals

4. Invest the remaining 10% for future growth

5. Track your expenses regularly using this app

6. Adjust categories as needed to stay within budget

Benefits

  • Simple to understand and implement
  • Balances present needs with future security
  • Encourages disciplined spending and saving
  • Provides clear financial priorities
  • Adaptable to different income levels
